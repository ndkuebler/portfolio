Concept walkthrough

Somni: a bedside dream-journal device

A nightstand device that captures and interprets your dream the moment you wake up — before the memory fades.

The problem

Dream content fades within five to ten minutes of waking. By the time you've found your phone, unlocked it, and opened an app, most of the recall window is gone. A phone app can't fix this — picking up the phone is the friction.

The architecture

  1. Bedside device. Always-on, listens for the iOS alarm, starts recording the moment it fires. No buttons, no unlock.
  2. Cloud analysis. Whisper transcribes; Claude extracts themes, emotions, and symbols.
  3. Web app for review. Dreams appear in a journal at breakfast, with cross-dream synthesis (recurring symbols, emotion trends, characters across weeks).

Key tradeoffs

  • Pi Zero 2 W over custom MCU. Overpowered, but runs full Python and ships USB audio. Right call for a capstone prototype.
  • Capacitive touch over physical buttons. Keeps the surface clean. Cost: calibration drift.
  • Silicone bottom + resin top. Two materials so the device grips the nightstand and doesn't feel like a 3D print.

The stability problem

The v5 black resin body (250 g) dominates the mass budget, and its back wall is thicker to host the hardware mounts — so the body's own centroid sits rearward. Add the 87 g hardware cluster on the same side and the combined center of mass falls within ~33 mm of the rear edge of a 110 mm-deep footprint. A nudge or cable tug tips it.

Counterweight sizing

Front-bottom pocket sits ~100 mm forward of the pivot. Goal: push the COM to roughly the geometric midpoint (~50 mm) so the device shrugs off a 1–2 N cable-pull or hand-bump.

Material choice

Requirements: dense (60 g needs to fit a small pocket), non-conductive (LED data and I²S clock run nearby), easy to install.

  • Tungsten putty — pick. ~10 g/cm³, so 60 g fits in ~6 cm³. Polymer binder insulates each particle, bulk resistance in the MΩ range. Conforms to the pocket; no fastener.
  • Lead shot (bagged) — fallback. Cheaper, but conductive and toxic; needs full encapsulation.
  • Hematite beads — alt. Fully non-conductive, ~5 g/cm³. 60 g needs ~12 cm³ — tight but feasible.
